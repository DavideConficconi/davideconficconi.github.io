About

I am a Post-Doctoral Researcher at Politecnico di Milano, where I got my Ph.D. in Information Technology. I am focusing on Reconfigurable Computing, particularly Field Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs) (from the optimized design to design automation), Domain-Specific Architecture and their abstraction stacks (from the compilers to the programming).

I am waiting for Martin to end "A Song of Ice and Fire." I am fond of Donato Carrisi books and popular physics books, such as those from Jim Al-Khalili and Carlo Rovelli. I also love to travel to discover new places and cultures.

Research Projects

TiReX

TiReX (a.k.a. Tiled Regular eXpressions matching architecture) is a Domain Specific Architecture for the Regular Expressions (REs) matching tasks. This work has been part of my, and my colleague and friend A. Comodi, Master Thesis in Computer Science and Engineering. Moreover with that project we have been finalist in the Xilinx Open HardWare competition in 2017. This work leads to two publication: "TiReX: Tiled Regular eXpression matching architecture." A. Comodi, D. Conficconi, A. Scolari, M. D. Santambrogio 2018 IEEE International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium Workshops (IPDPSW). IEEE, 2018. p. 131-137, and

D. Conficconi, E. Del Sozzo, F. Carloni, A. Comodi, A. Scolari, and M. D. Santambrogio. “An Energy-Efficient Domain-Specific Architecture for Regular Expressions.” IEEE Transactions on Emerging Topics in Computing (2022).

SDAccel-Chisel Integration

I collaborated with L. Di Tucci in the creation of a Chisel Wrapper for SDAccel RTL Flow, which is open source at this github repository. By means of this wrapper, every Chisel developer can emulate a kernel in a complete integrated way. This work is also part of a publication: "A Parallel, Energy Efficient Hardware Architecture for the merAligner on FPGA using Chisel HCL." L. Di Tucci, D.Conficconi, A. Comodi, S. Hofmeyr, D. Donofrio, M. D. Santambrogio 2018 IEEE International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium Workshops (IPDPSW). IEEE, 2018. p. 214-217.

BISMO

During my research stay at Xilinx in Dublin, I collaborated with the team of the BISMO project. BISMO is a programmable FPGA accelerator for few-bit integer matrix multiplication. This work leads to a publication: "Optimizing Bit-Serial Matrix Multiplication for Reconfigurable Computing." Y. Umuroglu, D.Conficconi, L. Rasnayake, T. Preusser, M. Själander 2019 ACM Transactions on Reconfigurable Technology and Systems (TRETS) 12.3 (2019): 1-24.

IRON

IRON is a framework, developed with E. D'Arnese, which enables both Image Registration and Mutual Information computations to FPGA acceleration. It is open sourced at this github repository, and it was a finalist of the Xilinx Open HardWare design contest 2019.

A Framework for Customizable FPGA-based Image Registration Accelerators

Starting from IRON, along with E. D'Arnese and E. Del Sozzo, we developed a framework for generating customizable FPGA-based accelerators of the MI computations, particularly devoted at Image Registration computation. Our paper has been accepted and published at the 2021 ACM/SIGDA International Symposium on Field-Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGA'21) and it is open-sourced at this github repository.

Xilinx Project Template

Together with E. Del Sozzo, we are developing a template repository for Xilinx-FPGA-based projects that starts from IRON as an example project. Our template provide an example of code organization, flows, utilities and makefiles for both Zynq-based (both SoC and MPSoC) devices and PCIe accelerator cards (e.g., Alveo cards), exploiting etiher PYNQ-based APIs or C/C++/OpenCL APIs. Currently focus on HLS-based, but we are working to extend for multi-code-base and provide some examples for RTL flows (IP packaging for both Vivado and Vitis flow). It is open-sourced at this github repository.

CICERO: A Domain-Specific Architecture for Efficient Regular Expression Matching

This project involve the idea of using Regular Expressions (RE) as instructions of a custom Domain Specific Architecture based on the Thompson's Algorithm and Russ Cox ideas. Mainly developed by D. Parravicini and together with E. Del Sozzo, we present an end-to-end framework for RE matching. We describe CICERO's approach in this article accepted at CASES'21 and published in ACM TODAES. It is open-sourced at this github repository.

Exploiting Heterogeneous Architectures for Rigid Image Registration

Mainly developed by E. D'Arnese and E. Del Sozzo, we present a framework for image registration that is able to exploit heterogeneous architectures with a particular focus on CPU-GPU ones. The ideas are described in paper accepted for publication at BioCAS 2021. It is open-sourced at this github repository.

cFp_Zoo

During my research stay at IBM in Zurich, I collaborated with the team of the cloudFPGA system. I mainly contributed to the open-source cFp_Zoo project, which is a cloudFPGA project with domain-specific accelerators for the hybrid multi-cloud era.

Dovado

Mainly developed by D. Paletti, we present a design space exploration framework, called Dovado for highly configurable RTL designs, ranging from small components to more advanced generators. Dovado is open-source at this github repository, and it has been published as: Paletti, Daniele, Davide Conficconi, and Marco D. Santambrogio. "Dovado: An Open-Source Design Space Exploration Framework." 2021 IEEE International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium Workshops (IPDPSW). IEEE, 2021.

Movado

Mainly developed by D. Paletti, along with F. Peverelli, we enhanced Dovado and developed Movado, an universal Fitness Function Approximator which learn onlines how to approximate expensive black-box functions such as RTL synthesis. Movado is open-source at this github repository, and it has been published as: Paletti, Daniele, Francesco Peverelli, and Davide Conficconi. "Online Learning RTL Synthesis for Automated Design Space Exploration." 2022 IEEE International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium Workshops (IPDPSW). IEEE, 2022.

Awesome FPGA Programming

This is a curated list of awesome languages and tools to program FPGAs and can be found this github repository. This list builds upon this survey: E. Del Sozzo, D. Conficconi, A. Zeni, M. Salaris, D. Sciuto, and M. D. Santambrogio. "Pushing the Level of Abstraction of Digital System Design: a Survey on How to Program FPGAs". ACM Computing Surveys (April 2022). https://doi.org/10.1145/3532989

Faber: a Hardware/Software Toolchain for Image Registration

Mainly developed by E. D'Arnese, E. Del Sozzo, and myself, we build Faber. Faber is an open-source HW/SW CAD framework tailored to image registration that exploits FPGAs as accelerators. The Faber toolchain comprises HW/SW highly-tunable registration components, supports users with different expertise in building custom pipelines, and automates the design process. Faber is open-sourced at this github repository and it is published as D'Arnese, E., Conficconi, D., Del Sozzo, E., Fusco, L., Sciuto, D., & Santambrogio, M. D. (2022). "Faber: a Hardware/Software Toolchain for Image Registration". IEEE Transactions on Parallel and Distributed Systems (TPDS).

Dovado

Mainly developed by R. Berzoini, we present SENECA to bring medical semantic segmentation to the edge with high energy efficiency and low segmentation time while preserving the accuracy. SENECA is open-source at this github repository, and it has been published as: Raffele Berzoini, Eleonora D'Arnese, and Davide Conficconi. "On How to Push Efficient Medical Semantic Segmentation to the Edge: the SENECA approach." 2022 IEEE International Parallel and Distributed Processing Symposium Workshops (IPDPSW). IEEE, 2022.

Dovado

Mainly developed by F. Peverelli, together with D. Bartolini and A. Scolari from Huawei Systems Laboratory Research Center, we present a characterization framework for LAMMPS (molecular dynamics simulation) on single nodes commodity platforms. We open-source our characterization and visualization framework at this github repository. We published the results of our experimental campaign as F. Peverelli, D. Conficconi, D. B. Bartolini, A. Scolari, M. D. Santambrogio. "Characterizing Molecular Dynamics Simulation on Commodity Platforms" 2022 IEEE International Symposium on Workload Characterization (IISWC).
